Affiliation

CBSE

Mayoor Chopasni School is authorised and affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education.
As an international school, we are now part of a global community of schools committed to developing knowledgeable, caring young individuals who will be ready to negotiate their future successfully and make contributions resulting in a more harmonious and peaceful world.
We hope to see the positive results of offering this wide range of courses, as batches of scholars graduate from Mayoor and undertake activities that enhance social, cultural and economic environments locally, nationally and perhaps, internationally.

CBSE – Central Board of Secondary Education

All scholars at Mayoor can pursue their learning in an uninhibited and non- restrictive manner.

Grade 10

The remodeled assessment structure effective from the Academic Year 2017- 18 now comprises of an 80 marks Board Examination and 20 marks of Internal Assessment that includes periodic tests, notebook submission and subject enrichment activities.

Grade 11 – Grade 12

The assessment is done on the basis of unit tests for Grade 11 as a regular feedback on academic performance.Grade 11 has Half Yearly and Annual Exams. Promotion to the next class depends on the performance in the annual exam, as per the passing criteria laid down by CBSE.Grade 12 has regular class tests, followed by half yearly exams. They have two test series examinations followed by Pre-Board Exams.Grade 12 appears for the All India Senior Secondary Certificate Examinations in March-April conducted by CBSE.

ISA

Mayoor Chopasni School's efforts to inculcate internationalism in its curriculum have been recognized and commended by the British Council. The school received the British Council International School Award (ISA) 2016-2019. In August 2016, Mayoor received ISA for the first time thereby greatly encouraging the students and staff. The award marks the efforts that Mayoor Chopasni School has consistently made to strengthen teaching in ways far afield from the conventional “chalk and talk” methodology to encourage students towards the formation of a global identity. After being awarded we will include more international activities in our school curriculum and in future we will organise student -teachers exchange programs and collaborative projects with country overseas. In order to spread internationalism in our city we will display our work to the other school representatives also.
